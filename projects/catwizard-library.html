Catwizard Library Custom Game Engine

Overview

This game and engine was a collaboration between Aidan, Trish, Max, Noah, Chris, and Madeline for an independant study course CSC 476 Real Time Computer Graphics taught by Dr. Zoë Wood with help from T.A's Claire and Srini. In only 10 weeks, we designed the gameplay, created the 3D models and animations, and built the custom game engine using OpenGL.

Engine

The custom game engine was built in C++ with OpenGL. The rendering engine included multiple shader effects like cat paw prints following the characters movement, deferred rendering for shading and shadows, screen effects of color filters, a start screen and death screen, a robust particle system, and all programmed gameplay elements. It also incorporated some lightweight header files like Miniaudio.h and Assimp asset importer to handle some of the resource managment.

Gameplay

The game is a 3D adventure roguelike game where you play the role of a cat wizard apprentice in a magical rearanging library. In each run, the layout of the room is randomized with bookshelves, tables, and other furnature.

In the room 3 types of aggressive elementals exist, and when aggrovated will approach with different movement patterns. The Ice elemental will slowly follow the players position, the lightning elemental will teleport towards the player on a cooldown, and the fire elemental will attempt to cut the player character off by moving in a spiral patter into the player.

To fend off the magical foes, the player has access to a dodge roll for burst movement, and is able to learn and accrue spells from magical glowing bookshelves scattered accross the randomly generating map. The spells come in a veriety of types, Fire, Ice, Electric, and Healing magic. The player is able to scroll through their current spell loadout to pick the best spell for the situation, as each enemy has a different weakness.

After defeating 3 enemies, the player collects 3 keys and can then enter the boss room where the magical stone golem resides. The boss has 3 phases including a projectile phase, a summoning phase, and a stomping phase. If you defeat the boss the next loop begins with a special shader visual effect. Each loop is harder with more enemies spawning each time.

My Role

Everyone on the team had to contribute in many ways, my primary role was that of a technical artist but there were many other pieces I contributed to the group project.

Skeletal Animation System

My primary contribution was incorporating out system for bone transformations with vertex weights so our character was able to be animated. There were several issues I ran into while creating this feature, mainly the transformations were not constistant between my animation software Blender and our game. In our game, the transformations would completely warp the character model, or take the character out of world space and distort it in object space, disconected from the camera. After weeks of debugging and reworking, the animation system worked and I was able to import, access, and play animations in realtime gameplay!

3D Modeling and Animation

This project was my first introduction to the process of 3D modeling. I have since had far more experience and would do a much better job, but this role included 10+ hours of learning Blender and practicing. I 3D modeled our main character and the elementals, including material values and animations exported into an fbx file and incorporated into the game. The characters were created to utilize a lowpoly aesthetic which I have since had much more experience in modeling.

Debugging Tools

Another large contribution to the project was the development of a debug camera mode that helped the whole team with fixing their changes. The camera disconects from the character and is given free flight movement, aswell as being able to reset the map generation, freeze the player character and enemy characters, and adjust the rendering effects like shadows and color filters.

Gameplay Design

The final most notable contribution was my additions to the gameplay design. The concept for this game came from a combination of the teams brainstorming ideas, like the cute cat library aesthetic and the roguelike elements, that I pitched and developed with the team.
